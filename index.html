LST and NDVI in Barcelona

To visualize the relation between low temperatures and greenery areas in Barcelona, two types of data were used:

  • The vegetation cover based on the NDVI index: available from Open Data BCN website.
  • The land surface temperature (LST): calculated using the thermal infrared imagery collected by the multispectral scanner of Landsat 8 satellite, 6th of July 2019, at 10:30 UTC when the temperature in Barcelona was 26ºC.

The estimated LST reaches a maximum temperature of 56.1ºC and a minimum of 27.3ºC. The highest temperatures are mainly concentrated in the industrial and commercial areas of the city. On the contrary, the parts with lower temperatures coincide with the vegetated areas of the city: especially corresponding to parks and gardens, but also wooded avenues and inner gardens of blocks.

This map has been created by Carla Garcia, Anna Peliova and Josep Sitjar, from Servei de SIG i Teledetecció (SIGTE) of the Universitat de Girona (UdG).

The visualization presented here shows how the areas with the lowest land surface temperature (LST) clearly coincide with the vegetated areas. By modifying the transparency of the vegetation cover layer you can see how this phenomenon happens even in the main wooded avenues. Likewise, the average LST and vegetation coverage layers shows how the most vegetated neighbourhoods are also the coolest ones.
The relationship between land surface temperatures (LST) and plant cover (NDVI) is very high, with an inversely proportional trend between the variables (R = -0.69), which means that the parts with lower temperatures coincide with the vegetated areas of the city.
